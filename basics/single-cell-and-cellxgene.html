2为什么要看"单个细胞"Why look at single cells

早年的测序是"bulk(混样)":把一块组织里成千上万个细胞打碎、一起测,得到的是这群细胞的平均值。问题是——一块"肺组织"里其实混着上皮细胞、免疫细胞、血管细胞…… 平均值会把它们抹平,看不出"是哪种细胞、在干什么"。 Classic sequencing was bulk: grind up a piece of tissue (thousands of cells) and sequence them together, giving the average over that cell population. The problem — a piece of "lung" actually mixes epithelial, immune, vascular… cells, and the average blends them so you can't tell which cell type is doing what.

一个比喻:bulk 像"把一锅汤搅匀尝一口";单细胞像"把汤里每一粒食材分开,逐个看清"。Analogy: bulk is "stir the soup and taste one spoon"; single-cell is "separate every ingredient and inspect each one".

3scRNA-seq:细胞 × 基因矩阵scRNA-seq: the cells × genes matrix

基因 → RNA → 蛋白:一个基因"开工"时会先转录成 mRNA。一个细胞里某基因的 mRNA 越多,通常说明这个基因在该细胞里"开得越大"。单细胞 RNA 测序把组织打散成一个个细胞,给每个细胞的 RNA 贴上独有的"条形码",再一起测序,最后按条形码把读到的 RNA 归回各自的细胞。结果就是一张大表: Gene → RNA → protein: when a gene is active it is transcribed into mRNA. The more mRNA of a gene in a cell, the more that gene is "on" there. scRNA-seq dissociates a tissue into single cells, tags each cell's RNA with a unique "barcode", sequences them together, and uses the barcodes to assign reads back to their cell of origin. The result is a big table:

            GeneA  GeneB  GeneC  ...   (每列一个基因 / one gene per column)
Cell_1        0      5      0    ...
Cell_2        3      0     12    ...
Cell_3        0      0      0    ...   (大多是 0 → 稀疏矩阵 / mostly zeros → sparse)
 ...
(每行一个细胞 / one cell per row)

这张 细胞 × 基因 表就是单细胞数据的核心。它通常很大(几万~几百万个细胞 × 两三万个基因)且稀疏(绝大多数格子是 0,因为任一细胞只表达一部分基因)。 This cells × genes table is the heart of single-cell data. It is usually large (tens of thousands to millions of cells × ~20–30k genes) and sparse (most entries are 0, since any one cell only expresses a subset of genes).

4深入:细胞怎么逐个隔离、贴码、扩增Deep dive: isolating, barcoding & amplifying single cells

上一节说"给每个细胞的 RNA 贴上独有条码"——这里把三种主流技术、以及"捕获/扩增"的化学原理讲透。The previous section said "tag each cell's RNA with a unique barcode" — here's how, across the three main methods, plus the capture/amplification chemistry.

① mRNA 怎么"被捕获到微珠上"?会不会跑到油里?① How is mRNA "captured onto the bead"? Does it go into the oil?

先纠正一个直觉:液滴是"油包水"——每个细胞、微珠、裂解液都在一个水滴里,油只是把一个个水滴彼此隔开,化学反应全在水相里发生。mRNA 是亲水的,不会进油。 First, a correction: the droplets are water-in-oil — each cell, bead, and lysis mix sits in a water droplet, and the oil only keeps droplets separate. All chemistry happens in the aqueous phase; mRNA is hydrophilic and does not enter the oil.

"捕获到微珠上"靠的是碱基互补配对:真核 mRNA 的 3′ 末端有一串 poly-A 尾(一长串 A)。微珠表面挂着几百万条 DNA 寡核苷酸,末端是 poly-T(一长串 T)。A 和 T 互补,mRNA 的 poly-A 尾就"钩"在微珠的 poly-T 上,被固定下来。 Capture is base-pairing: a eukaryotic mRNA has a poly-A tail (a run of A's) at its 3′ end. The bead is coated with millions of DNA oligos ending in poly-T (a run of T's). A pairs with T, so the mRNA's poly-A tail "hooks" onto the bead's poly-T and is held there.

不同水滴怎么带上不同标签?——关键在微珠。每颗微珠上的几百万条寡核苷酸都带同一段 cell barcode,但不同微珠带的是不同的 barcode(制珠时用"拆分-合成"让每颗珠子拿到唯一序列)。所以一个水滴分到哪颗珠,这滴里所有 mRNA 就被打上那颗珠的专属 barcode。不同水滴 = 不同珠 = 不同标签。标签不在油上,而在珠上。 How do different droplets get different labels? — it's the bead. On one bead, all millions of oligos share one cell barcode, but different beads carry different barcodes (split-and-pool bead synthesis gives each bead a unique sequence). So whichever bead a droplet gets, all mRNA in that droplet is stamped with that bead's barcode. Different droplet = different bead = different label. The label lives on the bead, not the oil.

那 barcode 又是怎么"长"到珠上的?不是贴上去的,而是用"拆分-合成(split-pool)"一段一段合成出来的——和 §4④ 给细胞贴码是同一套逻辑,只是用在造珠子上。以合成 12 碱基码为例:珠面本来密布几百万条一样的起始短链;第 1 轮把所有珠分到 4 个池(A/C/G/T),每池给珠上每条链都接上该池的碱基,再倒回混匀;重复 12 轮。同一颗珠每轮"进哪个池"是固定路径,所以它上面几百万条链接到的碱基完全一样 → 全珠同一个 barcode;不同珠走了不同路径 → 珠间不同。4¹²≈1670 万种,足够每颗珠近乎唯一。(10x 用预定的 barcode 白名单;Drop-seq 用随机合成、事后从数据反推。) How does the barcode get onto the bead? It isn't stuck on — it's synthesized segment by segment via split-pool, the same logic as the cell barcoding in §4④, but applied to bead manufacturing. For a 12-base barcode: the bead starts covered in millions of identical primer strands; round 1 splits all beads into 4 pools (A/C/G/T) and adds that pool's base to every strand on the beads there, then pools them back; repeat 12 rounds. One bead follows a fixed path of pools, so all its millions of strands receive the same base each round → one barcode per bead; different beads take different paths → different barcodes. 4¹² ≈ 16.7M combinations, enough to make each bead near-unique. (10x uses a predefined barcode whitelist; Drop-seq uses random synthesis and recovers barcodes from the data.)

② 液滴微流控(10x Genomics / Drop-seq)② Droplet microfluidics (10x Genomics / Drop-seq)

微流控芯片在交叉口把"细胞流""微珠流""油流"汇到一起,挤出一个个均匀油滴,目标是每滴恰好 1 个细胞 + 1 颗珠。做法是把细胞和珠稀释,让装载服从泊松分布——绝大多数油滴要么空、要么只 1 个细胞(牺牲一部分空滴,换取"极少出现 2 个细胞挤一滴")。这就是为什么"不需要事先分类型",只要"每滴至多一个细胞"即可。 A microfluidic chip merges a stream of cells, a stream of beads, and an oil stream at a junction, pinching off uniform droplets, aiming for exactly 1 cell + 1 bead per droplet. Cells and beads are diluted so loading follows a Poisson distribution — most droplets are empty or hold a single cell (you waste empty droplets to keep two-cell droplets rare). That's why no pre-sorting by type is needed: you only need "at most one cell per droplet".

"交叉口挤出液滴"具体怎么发生?——流动聚焦(flow-focusing)。芯片上有几条极细的通道:中间是水相(带着细胞和微珠往前流),两侧各来一股油,在一个很窄的收口处交汇。两股油从侧面把中间的水柱夹紧、剪断,水柱就一段段断成大小几乎一致的液滴(单分散),一个接一个、每秒几百到几千个地被"挤"进下游通道流走。液滴大小主要由通道几何 + 各路流速决定,所以能做得很均匀。 How exactly does the junction pinch off droplets? — flow-focusing. The chip has fine channels: the center carries the aqueous phase (cells + beads flowing forward), and two side channels bring oil that meets it at a narrow orifice. The two oil streams squeeze and shear the central aqueous thread, breaking it into near-identical droplets (monodisperse), one after another — hundreds to thousands per second — that flow off downstream. Droplet size is set mainly by channel geometry + the flow rates, so it's very uniform.

在 10x Genomics 里是"双交叉":先在一个交叉口把细胞与凝胶微珠(珠上挂着 barcode 寡核苷酸 + 反应试剂)汇到一起,紧接着遇到油被夹断,形成一个个含"1 细胞 + 1 珠 + 试剂"的液滴(叫 GEM)。进液滴后凝胶珠溶解,把几百万条带 barcode 的寡核苷酸释放出来捕获这个细胞的 mRNA。 In 10x Genomics it's a double junction: one junction first combines the cell with a gel bead (carrying the barcoded oligos + reagents), which then meets oil and is pinched into a droplet holding "1 cell + 1 bead + reagents" (a GEM). Inside, the gel bead dissolves, releasing its millions of barcoded oligos to capture that cell's mRNA.

两滴挨在一起,不会融合吗?——不会,靠"表面活性剂(乳化剂)"。从能量上看两滴合并能减小界面、是自然倾向,所以必须主动稳住。液滴用的油里掺了乳化剂——一种"一头亲水、一头亲油"的分子,它整齐铺在每个水滴表面、穿上一层"外衣"。两滴要合并得先把中间那层薄油膜挤干、捅破,而乳化剂①降低界面张力、②相互排斥,把油膜稳住不让它破 → 于是两滴只贴着、不融合。就像蛋黄(天然乳化剂)让沙拉酱里的油水稳定不分层。反过来:有些流程要往液滴补试剂时,得主动加电场逼它们合并——正说明"不干预就不会自融"。 Don't two touching droplets merge? — no, thanks to a surfactant (emulsifier). Merging reduces interfacial area and is the natural tendency, so droplets must be actively stabilized. The oil carries a surfactant — a molecule with one water-loving and one oil-loving end — that coats each droplet's surface like a skin. To merge, the thin oil film between two droplets must drain and rupture; the surfactant (1) lowers interfacial tension and (2) adds mutual repulsion that keeps the film from breaking, so droplets touch but don't fuse (like egg yolk, a natural emulsifier, keeping oil and water stable in mayonnaise). Conversely, workflows that want to add reagent to a droplet apply an electric field to force merging — proof that, left alone, they don't fuse.

③ 孔板 + FACS:细胞那么小,怎么分进单孔?③ Plates + FACS: how do tiny cells land in single wells?

FACS(流式细胞分选)的机制:FACS (Fluorescence-Activated Cell Sorting) works like this:

  • 排成单列:细胞悬液被挤过一个极细的喷嘴,靠"液流聚焦"让细胞一个接一个排成一条细流。Single file: the suspension is pushed through a fine nozzle; hydrodynamic focusing lines the cells up one after another in a thin stream.
  • 切成小滴:喷嘴高频振动,把液流打成大小一致的微滴,理想情况每滴 1 个细胞。Break into droplets: the nozzle vibrates to shear the stream into uniform droplets, ideally one cell each.
  • 逐个读取:每个细胞经过激光时,探测器读它的散射光(大小/颗粒度)和荧光(来自抗体标记,见 ⑥)。Read each cell: as a cell crosses a laser, detectors read its scatter (size/granularity) and fluorescence (from antibody labels, see ⑥).
  • 带电偏转:仪器根据读数瞬间给"含该细胞的液滴"充上电荷,液滴飞过两块高压偏转板时被电场拐向指定的孔/管(不想要的液滴不充电、直接进废液)。Charge & deflect: based on the reading, the instrument charges the droplet containing that cell; passing between high-voltage plates, the charged droplet is deflected into a chosen well/tube (unwanted droplets stay uncharged → waste).

这样每秒能分几千个细胞、精确落进 96/384 孔板的指定单孔(一孔一码)。既能"每孔一个细胞",也能顺便"只挑某一类"(见 ⑥)。 This sorts thousands of cells/second into specific single wells of a 96/384-well plate (one barcode per well). It gives "one cell per well", and can also "keep only a chosen type" (see ⑥).

细胞大小差别很大,液滴怎么"适配"?——其实不适配,也不用。液滴是固定大小的一滴鞘液(由喷嘴振动频率决定),而且比细胞大得多,细胞只是里面的小乘客——用大液滴装小细胞,大小对不上没关系。"每滴 1 个细胞"靠的是 ①稀释+排成单列拉开间距 ②液滴延迟(测到细胞→算准它落进哪一滴→只给那滴充电)③重合丢弃(两细胞太近就放弃这次分选保纯度),不是按细胞大小定制液滴。细胞大小(前向散射)只用来识别/门控;真正"按大小适配"的一步是操作员事先按细胞群体选喷嘴口径(大细胞用大口径、低压),一次设定、整轮固定(细胞比喷嘴大会堵)。液滴微流控同理。 Cells vary hugely in size — how does the droplet "adapt"? — it doesn't, and needn't. The droplet is a fixed-size drop of sheath fluid (set by the nozzle's vibration frequency) and is much larger than any cell — the cell is just a passenger, so a size mismatch is fine. "One cell per droplet" comes from (1) dilution + single-file spacing, (2) drop delay (detect a cell → compute which drop it will land in → charge only that drop), (3) coincidence abort (skip the sort if two cells are too close, to keep purity) — not from sizing droplets to cells. Cell size (forward scatter) is only used to identify/gate. The one real "size adaptation" is choosing a nozzle size upfront for the cell population (larger nozzle + lower pressure for big cells), fixed for the run (cells bigger than the nozzle clog it). Droplet microfluidics works the same way.
"流式仪(flow cytometer)"到底是什么?就是"让细胞排成单列、逐个流过激光、挨个测光信号"的仪器。只测不分的叫分析型;能测又能物理分选的叫分选型 / FACS。一秒可处理上万个细胞、同时读十几种荧光参数。可编程性:控制端主要靠厂商 GUI + 画"门(gating)"来选留哪些细胞(可配置、自动化中等,非通用 API);数据端很能编程——输出标准 FCS 文件,可用 FlowJo 或 Python(FlowKit)、R(flowCore)分析。价格:分析型约 $50k–$300k;分选型 FACS 约 $300k–$1M+,通常是核心平台共享设备 + 专职操作员(对比:10x Chromium 仪器约 $75k–$150k + 每样本试剂 ~$1–2k,成本模型不同)。 What is a "flow cytometer"? An instrument that lines cells up single-file, flows them past a laser, and reads each one's light signals. One that only measures is an analyzer; one that also physically separates cells is a sorter / FACS. It handles thousands of cells/second across a dozen-plus fluorescence parameters. Programmability: instrument control is mostly vendor GUI + drawing "gates" to choose which cells to keep (configurable, moderate automation, not a general API); the data side is very programmable — it outputs standard FCS files, analyzed in FlowJo or with Python (FlowKit) / R (flowCore). Price: analyzers ~$50k–$300k; sorters (FACS) ~$300k–$1M+, usually a shared core-facility instrument with a trained operator (vs. a 10x Chromium at ~$75k–$150k + ~$1–2k/sample in kits — a different cost model).

④ 组合索引(split-pool,如 sci-RNA-seq / SPLiT-seq)④ Combinatorial indexing (split-pool; e.g. sci-RNA-seq / SPLiT-seq)

前两种都要"物理隔离每个细胞"(液滴/单孔),需要专门仪器、通量受限。组合索引换个思路——不隔离单细胞,而是把细胞本身当容器(先固定/透膜,让分子留在各自细胞内),靠"多轮贴码"给每个细胞一个独一无二的条码组合: The first two require physically isolating each cell (droplets/wells) — special hardware, limited throughput. Combinatorial indexing instead never isolates single cells: it uses each cell as its own container (fix/permeabilize so molecules stay inside), and gives every cell a unique combination of barcodes over several rounds:

  • 第 1 轮:把所有细胞分到 96 个孔,每个孔贴上不同的"码 1",然后全部倒回一起混匀。Round 1: split all cells into 96 wells, each well adds a different "barcode-1", then pool everything back together.
  • 第 2 轮:再随机分到 96 个孔(细胞重新洗牌),每孔贴"码 2",再混合。Round 2: split again into 96 wells (cells reshuffled), each adds "barcode-2", pool again.
  • 重复 N 轮。每个细胞最终携带的"码 1 + 码 2 + …"组合,由它每轮"进了哪个孔"决定。因为每轮都随机洗牌,两个细胞走出完全相同路径的概率极低 → 组合几乎唯一。Repeat N rounds. A cell's final "barcode-1 + barcode-2 + …" is set by which well it entered each round. Since cells reshuffle every round, two cells taking the exact same path is very unlikely → the combination is near-unique.

数学很划算:96 孔 × 3 轮 = 96³ ≈ 88 万种组合,用区区 96×3 个码就能给几十万个细胞各贴唯一标签,完全不需要单细胞隔离设备。只要装载的细胞数远少于组合数,"两个细胞撞同一组合"的概率就很低。 The math is efficient: 96 wells × 3 rounds = 96³ ≈ 884,000 combinations — just 96×3 barcodes uniquely tag hundreds of thousands of cells, with no single-cell isolation hardware. Load far fewer cells than combinations and "two cells, same combo" stays rare.

⑤ 为什么要 PCR 扩增?UMI 又是干嘛的?⑤ Why PCR-amplify? And what is the UMI for?

PCR(聚合酶链式反应)是"把一段 DNA 复制出很多份"的方法:靠反复的温度循环,每循环大约让数量翻倍。一个循环 3 步: PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) copies a DNA stretch many times using repeated temperature cycles, roughly doubling the count each cycle. One cycle has 3 steps:

  • 变性(denaturation,~95℃):加热让双链 DNA 的两条链分开(碱基对之间的氢键断开)→ 得到两条单链模板。Denaturation (~95°C): heat splits the double helix into two single strands (the base-pair H-bonds break) → two template strands.
  • 引物退火(annealing,~50–65℃):降温,让一小段人工合成的 引物(primer,约 20 个碱基,序列与目标两端互补)配对贴到单链上,标出"从这里开始复制"。Primer annealing (~50–65°C): cool so short synthetic primers (~20 bases, complementary to the target ends) base-pair onto the single strands, marking where copying starts.
  • 聚合酶延伸(extension,~72℃):DNA 聚合酶从引物起,沿模板逐个接上 A/T/G/C,合成出互补的新链 → 每条单链又变回双链。(用耐高温的 Taq 聚合酶,才能扛住 95℃ 的变性步骤。)Extension (~72°C): a DNA polymerase starts at each primer and adds A/T/G/C along the template, building the complementary new strand → each single strand becomes double again. (A heat-stable polymerase like Taq is used so it survives the 95°C step.)

重复约 30 轮:1 → 2 → 4 → …,2³⁰ ≈ 十亿份(指数放大)。"链式反应"之名,就是因为每轮的产物又成了下一轮的模板。 Repeat ~30 cycles: 1 → 2 → 4 → …, 2³⁰ ≈ a billion copies (exponential). It's a "chain reaction" because each cycle's products become templates for the next.

为什么必须扩增:一个细胞里的 RNA 极其微量(皮克级,只有几十万条 mRNA 分子),远不够测序仪检测。所以先把 mRNA 逆转录成更稳定的 cDNA,再 PCR 扩增到足够量才能上机测序。 Why it's needed: one cell holds a tiny amount of RNA (picograms; only a few hundred thousand mRNA molecules) — far too little for a sequencer. So mRNA is reverse-transcribed to sturdier cDNA and PCR-amplified to a sequenceable amount.

代价 → 所以有 UMI:PCR 对不同分子放大得不均匀(有的被多复制),直接数读数会失真。UMI(唯一分子标识符)在扩增之前就给每一条原始 mRNA 分子贴一个随机短码;测完后,把"同一 cell barcode + 同一基因 + 同一 UMI"的读数合并成 1 条,就还原出真实的原始分子数,抵消了 PCR 的重复偏差。 The catch → hence UMIs: PCR amplifies molecules unevenly (some get copied more), so raw read counts are distorted. A UMI (Unique Molecular Identifier) tags each original mRNA molecule with a random short code before amplification; afterwards, reads sharing "same cell barcode + same gene + same UMI" are collapsed to one, recovering the true original molecule count and cancelling PCR-duplication bias.

⑥ 抗体分选:FACS 与 MACS 怎么"按类型"挑细胞⑥ Antibody sorting: how FACS & MACS pick cells "by type"

如果你确实想先按类型富集(而不是测全部),靠的是抗体 + 表面标志物:If you do want to enrich a type first (rather than profile everything), it's done with antibodies + surface markers:

  • 表面标志物:不同细胞类型在细胞膜上挂着不同的蛋白组合(常记作 CD 编号,如 T 辅助细胞是 CD3+CD4+)。Surface markers: different cell types display different combinations of membrane proteins (often "CD" numbers, e.g. helper T cells are CD3+CD4+).
  • 抗体:能高度专一地结合某个标志物的蛋白。给抗体挂上一个"标记",就能认出/挑出带该标志物的细胞。Antibodies: proteins that bind one marker very specifically. Attach a "label" to the antibody and you can recognize/pick cells bearing that marker.
  • FACS:给抗体挂荧光染料 → 细胞被点亮成某种颜色 → 流式仪按颜色逐个分选(见 ③),能同时看多个标志物、做精细"门控"。FACS: label the antibody with a fluorescent dye → cells light up in a color → the sorter separates them one by one by color (see ③), reading several markers at once for fine "gating".
  • MACS(磁珠分选):给抗体挂微型磁珠 → 混样流过置于磁场中的柱子,带标记的细胞被吸住留下、其余流走;撤磁场再把目标细胞冲下来。设备便宜、快,但只能做"结合/不结合"的二分富集,不如 FACS 精细。MACS (Magnetic-Activated Cell Sorting): label the antibody with a tiny magnetic bead → run the mix through a column in a magnetic field; labeled cells are retained, the rest flow through; remove the magnet to release the target cells. Cheap and fast, but only a bind/no-bind split — less fine-grained than FACS.
为什么细胞会有这些表面标志物?因为这些膜蛋白首先是"干活的",不是标签——受体(收信号)、黏附分子(粘邻居)、通道/转运体(运物质)、免疫识别分子等,都露在细胞表面这个"对外界面"上(所以抗体够得着、还不用杀死细胞)。而不同细胞类型开不同的基因(§5),就挂出不同的膜蛋白组合 → 这套组合成了它身份的"指纹";我们只是借它当标签来认。CD(Cluster of Differentiation,分化群)是国际上给这些表面分子编的统一目录。有些标志物还随细胞成熟/激活出现或消失,所以也能标"状态"。 Why do cells have these surface markers? Because these membrane proteins are first of all functional, not labels — receptors (receive signals), adhesion molecules (stick to neighbors), channels/transporters, immune-recognition molecules — all sitting on the cell's outward-facing surface (so antibodies can reach them without killing the cell). Since different cell types express different genes (§5), they display different membrane-protein combinations → that combination becomes an identity fingerprint, which we merely borrow as a marker. CD (Cluster of Differentiation) is the standardized catalog of these surface molecules. Some markers appear/disappear as cells mature or activate, so they also track state.

5同一套 DNA,细胞为何各不相同Same DNA, different cells

这是最关键的生物学直觉:你身上几乎所有细胞的 DNA 都一样(同一套基因组)。神经元、肌肉细胞、免疫细胞的不同,不来自"基因不同",而来自 基因表达的不同——每种细胞选择性地开一批基因、关另一批。 This is the key intuition: nearly all your cells share the same DNA (one genome). A neuron, a muscle cell, and an immune cell differ not because their genes differ, but because their gene expression differs — each cell type selectively turns some genes on and others off.

  • 细胞类型(cell type):相对稳定的身份,如"T 细胞""肺上皮细胞"。由一套特征基因(marker)的表达定义。Cell type: a relatively stable identity (e.g. "T cell", "lung epithelial cell"), defined by a set of marker genes it expresses.
  • 细胞状态(cell state):同一类型的暂时状态,如"激活的""正在分裂的""受感染的"。Cell state: a transient condition of the same type — "activated", "dividing", "infected".
  • 怎么认出类型:把成千上万个细胞按表达谱的相似度聚类,每一簇往往对应一种细胞类型;再看每簇高表达的 marker 基因给它命名。How types are found: cells are clustered by similarity of their expression profiles; each cluster usually maps to a cell type, named by its high marker genes.
  • 为什么有"稀有类型":一块组织里各类细胞数量差别极大——有的占多数,有的可能不到 1%(如干细胞、各种祖细胞、罕见免疫亚群)。这些稀有类型往往功能关键(如再生组织的干细胞、疾病起始细胞),却会在 bulk 平均里被彻底淹没。为什么天然存在:发育是一棵"干细胞 → 祖细胞 → 各种成熟细胞"的树,有些是过渡态、有些被刻意维持在极少数量(如干细胞若太多会失控增生)。单细胞逐个测,哪怕几十个细胞聚成一簇也能被发现 → 这就是"能发现没预期到的稀有类型"的由来。 Why "rare types" exist: within a tissue, cell types differ hugely in abundance — some dominate, others may be under 1% (stem cells, various progenitors, rare immune subsets). These are often functionally critical (tissue-regenerating stem cells, disease-initiating cells) yet are erased by a bulk average. Why they exist: development is a tree "stem cell → progenitor → many mature types", with some transient states and some deliberately kept few (too many stem cells → uncontrolled growth). Profiling every cell means even a few dozen cells forming a distinct cluster can be spotted → hence "discovering unexpected rare types".
所以单细胞数据不仅是一张表——它让你能问:"肺里有几种细胞?每种占多少?某个基因(如 ACE2)主要在哪类细胞里表达?"这类问题 bulk 答不了。So single-cell data isn't just a table — it lets you ask: "how many cell types are in the lung? in what proportions? which cell type mainly expresses a given gene (e.g. ACE2)?" — questions bulk can't answer.

6细胞图谱与 CZ CELLxGENE CensusCell atlases & CZ CELLxGENE Census

全世界实验室做了海量单细胞实验(不同组织、物种、疾病)。细胞图谱(cell atlas)的目标是把它们汇总成"人体(或小鼠…)所有细胞类型的参考地图"。但各家数据格式、基因命名、细胞类型标注五花八门,难以直接合用。 Labs worldwide have produced enormous numbers of single-cell experiments (many tissues, species, diseases). A cell atlas aims to combine them into a reference "map of all cell types" of the human (or mouse…) body. But datasets differ wildly in format, gene naming, and cell-type labels, so they can't be used together as-is.

CZ CELLxGENE(Chan Zuckerberg Initiative 主导)做两件事:①一个门户,把大量单细胞数据集标准化(统一的元数据字段、基因 ID、细胞类型本体)后集中托管、可在线浏览;②Census —— 把这些标准化数据整合成一个可编程查询的大对象:你不用下载整个数据集,而是"按条件切一块"(如"人 + 肺 + 某几种细胞类型 + 某几个基因")直接拿回内存。 CZ CELLxGENE (led by the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative) does two things: (1) a portal that standardizes many single-cell datasets (uniform metadata fields, gene IDs, a cell-type ontology) and hosts them for browsing; (2) the Census — those standardized data integrated into one programmatically queryable object: instead of downloading whole datasets, you "slice by criteria" (e.g. "human + lung + a few cell types + a few genes") straight into memory.

版本很重要:Census 有带日期的版本号(如 2023-05-15)。固定版本 → 查询结果可复现。这也是 gget cellxgene 有 census_version 参数的原因。Versioning matters: the Census has dated versions (e.g. 2023-05-15). Pin a version → reproducible queries. That's why gget cellxgene has a census_version argument.

7数据长什么样:AnnDataWhat the data looks like: AnnData

单细胞领域用一个标准容器 AnnData(annotated data,"带注释的数据")来装那张细胞×基因表 + 所有旁注。核心结构: The field uses a standard container, AnnData (annotated data), to hold the cells × genes table plus all its annotations. Core structure:

AnnData是什么What it is
X表达矩阵本身:行=细胞、列=基因(n_obs × n_vars)the expression matrix: rows=cells, cols=genes (n_obs × n_vars)
obs每个细胞的元数据表(一行一个细胞):组织、细胞类型、疾病、性别、供体…per-cell metadata (one row per cell): tissue, cell type, disease, sex, donor…
var每个基因的信息表(一行一个基因):基因 ID、基因名、长度…per-gene info (one row per gene): gene ID, gene name, length…
layers同一 X 的其它版本(如原始计数 vs 归一化后),键=层名alternate versions of X (e.g. raw counts vs normalized), keyed by layer name
obsm / uns细胞的降维坐标(如 UMAP)/ 杂项非结构化注释cell embeddings (e.g. UMAP) / miscellaneous unstructured annotations
串回我们刚修的 CI bug(PR #266 / issue #265):gget 的测试用 repr_dict() 把一个 AnnData "拍平"成结构字典(记录 obs/var/layers 各有哪些键)来和期望值比对。cellxgene-census 某次更新后,返回的 AnnData 的 layers 里多出一个键为 None 的空层,于是结构字典里凭空多了 layers: [None]、测试就挂了。修法:让 repr_dict 忽略 None 键(那是噪声,不是有意义的层)。—— 现在你知道 layers 在生物学上是什么了,这个 bug 也就不神秘了。 Tying back to the CI bug we just fixed (PR #266 / issue #265): gget's test uses repr_dict() to flatten an AnnData into a structure dict (which keys each of obs/var/layers has) and compare it to an expected value. After a cellxgene-census update, the returned AnnData's layers gained a spurious None-keyed empty layer, so the structure dict grew a bogus layers: [None] and the test broke. The fix: make repr_dict ignore None keys (that's noise, not a meaningful layer). Now that you know what layers means biologically, the bug isn't mysterious.

8gget cellxgene 做什么What gget cellxgene does

gget cellxgene 是 CZ CELLxGENE Census 的一个便捷接口:你给出筛选条件(物种、组织、细胞类型、基因、Census 版本…),它就从图谱里"切"出你要的那块,返回一个 AnnData(表达矩阵 + 注释),或只返回元数据(不含表达矩阵、更轻)。 gget cellxgene is a convenience interface to the CZ CELLxGENE Census: you give filter criteria (species, tissue, cell type, gene, Census version…) and it "slices" that piece out of the atlas, returning an AnnData (matrix + annotations) or just the metadata (no matrix, lighter).

# Python — 取一小块 AnnData
import gget
adata = gget.cellxgene(
    tissue="lung",
    cell_type=["mucus secreting cell", "neuroendocrine cell"],
    gene=["ACE2", "ABCA1", "SLC5A1"],
    census_version="2023-05-15",
)
# adata.n_obs 个细胞 × adata.n_vars 个基因;adata.obs 里有 tissue/cell_type 等
单细胞数据可以非常大:不加限制地取一个组织的全部细胞,可能是几十万行 × 两万列。务必用 tissue/cell_type/gene 收窄,或先只取 meta_only 元数据看看规模。 Single-cell data can be very large: an unrestricted pull of a whole tissue can be hundreds of thousands of rows × ~20k columns. Narrow with tissue/cell_type/gene, or fetch meta_only metadata first to gauge the size.
