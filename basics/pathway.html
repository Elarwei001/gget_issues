TL;DR： 通路 = 细胞里一条分工协作、可调控的"分子流水线"：一组蛋白/基因一步接一步完成某个任务。细胞靠它办事（造能量、传信号、合成分子）；缺了会失能，失调会致病（癌症、代谢病等）。区分通路主要看"它由哪组基因组成"——而 MitoCarta、MSigDB 这些数据库正是这些基因组成的权威名录。 A pathway is a coordinated, regulated "molecular assembly line" inside the cell: a group of proteins/genes that complete a task step by step. Cells use pathways to get things done (make energy, relay signals, build molecules); losing one causes loss of function, and mis-regulating one causes disease (cancer, metabolic disorders). Pathways are told apart mainly by which genes make them up — and databases like MitoCarta and MSigDB are the authoritative catalogs of those gene memberships.

1通路是什么What a pathway is

通路 = 细胞里一条"流水线"：一组分工协作的分子（主要是蛋白质/酶，由基因编码），一步接一步地完成某个特定任务。不是单个分子单打独斗，而是接力——第一步的产物是第二步的原料，像工厂里"零件 → 工位1加工 → 工位2组装 → 成品"。每个"工位"通常是一个蛋白质/酶，对应一个基因。 A pathway is an "assembly line" in the cell: a group of cooperating molecules (mostly proteins/enzymes encoded by genes) that complete a specific task step by step. It's a relay, not a solo act — the product of step 1 is the input to step 2, like a factory "part → station 1 → station 2 → finished product." Each "station" is usually one protein/enzyme, encoded by one gene.

细胞要干的活很多，每类活基本都由某条通路负责。大致两类：Cells have many jobs, and each kind of job is handled by some pathway. Broadly two types:

3没有会怎么样What happens without it

某条通路缺失/断了 → 细胞做不了那件事：If a pathway is missing/broken, the cell can't do that job:

  • 断的是能量通路（如 OXPHOS）→ 细胞"断电"，最耗能的器官（脑、心、肌肉）先出问题——这正是线粒体病。If it's an energy pathway (e.g. OXPHOS) → the cell loses "power," and the most energy-hungry organs (brain, heart, muscle) fail first — this is mitochondrial disease.
  • 断的是发育信号通路 → 胚胎发育不出来（常常致死）。If it's a developmental signaling pathway → the embryo fails to develop (often lethal).
  • 一句话：通路是"必需工序"，少了对应功能就瘫痪，轻则细胞失能，重则致病/致死。In short: a pathway is a "required step"; without it the function collapses — mild cases lose function, severe cases cause disease or death.

4出问题会怎么样（更常见、更要命）What happens when it goes wrong (more common, more serious)

通路平时是被严格调控的（该开时开、该关时关）。出问题往往不是"没了"，而是卡在开或卡在关：Pathways are normally tightly regulated (on when needed, off otherwise). The problem is usually not "gone" but stuck on or stuck off:

  • 代谢通路某步的酶坏了 → 原料堆积、产物缺失 → 代谢病（如 PKU：缺一个酶，苯丙氨酸堆积毒害大脑）。An enzyme in a metabolic step breaks → inputs pile up, products go missing → metabolic disease (e.g. PKU: a missing enzyme lets phenylalanine build up and harm the brain).
  • 生长信号通路卡在"开"（本该收到信号才生长，却永远在喊"生长"）→ 细胞无限增殖 → 癌症。很多癌症本质就是某条信号通路突变、关不掉。A growth-signaling pathway stuck "on" (it should only grow when signaled, but keeps shouting "grow") → cells divide without limit → cancer. Many cancers are, at heart, a signaling pathway mutated so it can't turn off.
  • 细胞凋亡("该死就死")通路坏了 → 本该自杀的坏细胞不死 → 也致癌；反过来死太多 → 神经退行病。The apoptosis ("die on command") pathway breaks → bad cells that should self-destruct don't → also cancer; conversely too much death → neurodegeneration.
核心：疾病常常不是"某个基因坏了"这么简单，而是"某条通路失调了"——这也是研究者极其关心通路的原因。Key insight: disease is often not simply "one gene broke" but "a whole pathway is dysregulated" — which is exactly why researchers care so much about pathways.

5如何区分不同的通路How to tell pathways apart

  1. 干什么活：造能量 vs 传信号 vs 合成物质。What job it does: make energy vs relay signals vs synthesize molecules.
  2. 由哪些基因/蛋白组成——每条通路有一组特征成员基因。⭐ 这就是 MitoPathways、MSigDB 合集在编目的东西：一条通路 = 一个"有名字的基因集合"。Which genes/proteins make it up — each pathway has a characteristic set of member genes. ⭐ This is exactly what MitoPathways and MSigDB collections catalog: a pathway = a "named set of genes."
  3. 在哪发生：线粒体 / 细胞质 / 细胞核。Where it happens: mitochondria / cytoplasm / nucleus.
  4. 层级归属：大类 → 子通路 → 具体步骤，如 OXPHOS > Complex I > CI subunits（能量总线 ▸ Complex I 机器 ▸ 它的零件）。Hierarchy: big category → sub-pathway → specific step, e.g. OXPHOS > Complex I > CI subunits (energy line ▸ the Complex I machine ▸ its parts).

7这就是 gget mitocarta / enrichr 存在的意义Why gget mitocarta / enrichr exist

研究者天天问："我实验测出一堆异常基因，它们是不是集中在某条已知通路上？"（通路富集分析）。要回答它，就得有"哪些基因属于哪条通路"的权威名录——MitoPathways（线粒体）和 MSigDB 合集（通用）正是这些名录。所以 gget mitocarta --which pathways 和 gget enrichr --get_library 本质就是帮研究者把"某条通路里有哪些基因"这份名录拉下来，好去做富集分析。 Researchers constantly ask: "my experiment flagged a bunch of abnormal genes — are they concentrated in a known pathway?" (pathway enrichment analysis). Answering it needs an authoritative catalog of "which genes belong to which pathway" — and MitoPathways (mitochondria) and MSigDB collections (general) are exactly those catalogs. So gget mitocarta --which pathways and gget enrichr --get_library essentially pull down the roster of "which genes are in a pathway" so you can run enrichment analysis.
